Chocolate Chip Cookies

  • Home

1 cup of margarine or butter

1 cup of brown sugar

½ cup of white, granulated sugar

2 cups of flour

1 tsp baking soda

1 tsp salt

2 cups of chocolate chips

2 eggs

2 tsp vanilla extract

Parchment paper is great. You can just slide the paper off of the cookie sheet onto the counter for cooling.

I usually use margarine because it’s soft and butter is usually hard. If you want butter and you’re thinking ahead, I imagine you could leave the butter out of the fridge so that it’s soft. As for me, I usually make them spur of the moment, so margarine works best for me. I have no idea if it makes much of a difference flavour-wise.

I like the darker brown sugar. Maybe I’ve used demerara. Maybe it was some that was darker from more molasses or something. But I’m not so picky and I’ll make them with whatever brown sugar I have on hand.

I use a full teaspoon of salt, but I’ve made cookies for people who think less salt is healthier. They’re cookies. I’m not so concerned about the healthiness of my cookies. If you want, use three grains of salt and substitute broccoli for the chocolate chips.

I’ve made the cookies with gluten-free flour before. Usually that means having to increase the amount of flour. That’s an experiment every time. I didn’t do it enough to nail down the exact increase.

Cream together the margarine, the brown sugar, and the white sugar.

Mix the eggs and the vanilla extract in with the margarine and sugar mix.

Mix in the flour, salt, and baking soda.

Add the chocolate chips (or broccoli chunks or M&Ms)

Drop the cookie dough from a spoon onto the parchment paper on the cookie sheet(s). I often fit two cookie sheets on the oven rack.

Bake at 350 degrees Fahrenheit for about 12 minutes.
